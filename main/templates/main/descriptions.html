Quarterback

Arm Strength: Ball velocity, trajectory, and distance on a quarterbacks throws

Size: Height and weight quantified

Short Accuracy: Accuracy on throws generally inside of 10 yards. Short throws are mainly judged on how well the quarterback hits his receiver in stride, allowing him to easily catch and create run after catch opportunities.

Deep Accuracy: Quarterbacks ability to throw the ball deep and to intermediate levels (10-30 yards). Ability to layer throws over and in front of defenders. Throws receivers open, away from leverage, or allows for easy tracks.

Mobility: The Quarterbacks ability to sense and evade pressure. Pocket manipulation and presceince. Ability to create favorable situations in and out of structure.

Processing: Ability to process defensive coverage pre and post snap. Quickly going through progressions and ability to read the entire field.

Decicion Making: Mainly the ratio in which a quarterback takes risks to make big plays and avoid turnovers

Athleticism: Speed and movement skills.

Mechanics: Footwork and release time on all throws

Timing and Anticipation: Ability to throw at the ideal time and anticipate leverage. Rhythm.

Ball Carrying: Running ability with the ball in hands exclusive to any passing play. Essentially a general grade for runnning back traits.

Runningback

Size: Height and weight quantified

Vision: Ability to read running lanes and maximize yardage based off of whats given.

Balance: Ability to maintain balance through contact. Ability to break tackles.

Elusiveness: Ability to evade defenders and tacklers in the open field and short areas.

Speed Straightline speed. Quantified workout numbers and 'game speed'.

Burst: Ability to accellerate given a rushing lane and how quickly a runningback can get to top speed.

Pass blocking: Ability to process blitzing defenders, anchor and maintain blocks in the pass game.

Mechanics: Footwork and release time on all throws

Fumble: Ball security and how often a runningback fumbles.

Change of Direction: Ability to quickly change running direction.

Wide Receiver

Size: Height and weight quantified

Speed Straightline speed. Quantified workout numbers and 'game speed'.

Change of Direction: Ability to quickly change running direction.

Release: Ability to create initial seperation off the line of scrimmage against press coverage.

Short Route Running: Ability to create seperation on routes that break within 5 yards.

Deep Route Running: Ability to create seperation on intermediate and deep routes, and the ability to stem off of them. Ball tracking also plays a big factor in this trait.

Hands: Ability to bring a pass in that is in a catchable location consistently.

Catch In Traffic: Ability to catch a pass through contact.

Ball Skills Body control and ability to attack the football in air. Leverage and physicality at the catch point.

Run After Catch Running ability with the ball in hands. Essentially a general grade for runnning back traits.

Blocking: Ability to sustain blocks and move defenders off of their spot in the run game. More emphasis on run blocking.
