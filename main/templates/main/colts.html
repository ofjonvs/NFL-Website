Indianapolis Colts

Quarterback: the team keeps reaching at quarterbacks proven to be “good” in the past because it's the safer choice than taking a quarterback who doesn't pan out. The team has to take a young quarterback or figure out some alternative that is good enough not to waste the roster. Even a game manager like Jared Goff or Jimmy Garrapolo would be a good enough fix.

Offensive Line: The colts offensive line forgot how to play football this season. Rookie Bernhard Raimann at LT has shown loads of promise so I don't think you could designate blame to him for the lines struggles. Braden Smith is on a recently signed large contract extension and hasn't been awful. The right guard position was left thin all off-season and the consequences showed all year, as well as a GIANT regression from center Ryan Kelly that left even Quenton Nelson's play taking a notable decline. This is the IMMEDIATE number one priority after you decide who the quarterback is going to be.

-John Davis
